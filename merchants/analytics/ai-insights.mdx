The AI Insights Panel reads your loyalty activity and generates observations about it without you having to ask. Recommendations, trend summaries, short-term forecasts, and unusual patterns all appear in one place at the bottom of the Analytics Overview page.

To get there: go to **Analytics** in the left sidebar and scroll past the main metrics and charts.

## The four sections

The panel breaks its output into four separate cards. A card only appears when there is data worth showing. An empty program will see nothing here until activity builds up.

<Frame caption="What each section looks like once your program has data. From top left: Recommendations, Trends, Next 7 days, Worth a look.">
  <img src="/images/analytics/ai-insights-layout.svg" alt="Diagram of the four AI Insights Panel sections" />
</Frame>

### Recommendations

Each recommendation shows a title, an impact rating (high, medium, or low), an effort rating (easy, moderate, or significant), and a category (Retention, Growth, Revenue, or Engagement). Below the headline is a short explanation of the suggestion. A "Why it matters" note ties it to your actual numbers, and an expected outcome tells you what acting on it might change.

When the badge reads **AI-powered**, the suggestions are drawing on your real transaction data rather than default templates.

### Trends

A row for each key metric showing direction (up, down, or stable), percentage change, and a one-line context note. The context note compares your movement to what is typical for programs of your type, so you can tell whether a drop is unusual or just normal variance.

### Next 7 days

Short-range forecast cards based on your recent patterns. Each card shows a predicted number and a confidence level (high, medium, or low), with a one-line explanation of what drove the forecast. Two cards appear side by side on wider screens.

### Worth a look

Spikes, drops, or other unusual readings the panel flagged. Each card shows the metric, the date it happened, what the panel expected versus what it saw, a likely cause, and a plain-language suggestion for what to do about it.

The severity of each anomaly is marked as **info**, **warning**, or **critical**. Info items are curiosities worth knowing about. Critical items usually need attention before your next busy period.

## Time period

The panel uses the same time period selected at the top of the page. Switching from **Last 30 days** to **Last 7 days** refreshes all four sections. Results are cached for five minutes, so rapid back-and-forth on the time selector will show the cached version until the window expires.

## When the panel stays empty

If your program is new or has had very little activity, the panel will not render. There is nothing wrong with the account. It just needs a baseline of transactions before it can spot patterns. Once stamps or points start coming in regularly, the panel begins filling in.

If recommendations appear but feel generic ("reach out to inactive members"), your account is probably below the threshold for fully personalized suggestions. A few more weeks of activity will sharpen them.

## The AI Insight Pack

The AI Insight Pack add-on increases how frequently and in how much depth the panel generates insights. It costs $9 per 50 insights and is available on **Elite** and **Franchise** plans. To add it, go to **Settings → Billing** and look for Add-ons.

<Info>
The panel generates a standard set of insights on all paid plans. The add-on gives you more of them, updated more often.
</Info>

## Acting on what you see

Each recommendation, prediction, and anomaly card has an action button. It links directly to Members, Campaigns, Cards, Notifications, or Analytics depending on what the insight is about. Use those buttons rather than navigating separately.

For anomalies, the "What to do" note tells you in plain language what to check. Most suggestions are things like confirming a campaign ran or checking whether a staff member scanned cards that day.

For further context on the underlying metrics the panel draws from, see [Analytics Overview](/merchants/analytics/overview).
