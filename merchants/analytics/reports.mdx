The Detailed Reports section lives inside Analytics and gives you a closer look at specific parts of your loyalty data. Open Analytics from the left sidebar and click **Detailed Reports** in the sub-navigation, or go to `Analytics → Reports` in the dashboard.

<Frame caption="The Detailed Reports hub with links to all four reports.">
  <img src="/images/analytics/reports-hub-final.png" alt="Detailed Reports hub showing four report cards" />
</Frame>

Each report has its own time period selector. Changing it on one report does not affect the others or the main Analytics overview.

## Points Activity

A paginated transaction log showing every point that moved in your program during the selected period. Each row shows the date, member name, transaction type (Earned, Redeemed, Adjusted, or Expired), the point amount, the associated purchase amount if one was recorded, and a description. Pages load 50 rows at a time.

<Frame caption="Points Activity report showing transactions with type badges and point amounts.">
  <img src="/images/analytics/points-activity-final.png" alt="Points Activity report transaction table" />
</Frame>

This is your audit trail. If a member disputes their balance, the Points Activity report is where you check what actually happened.

## Revenue Impact

Shows how loyalty transaction volume tracks against revenue over time. Three summary cards at the top give you total revenue, total loyalty transactions, and the average revenue per transaction for the period. Below them, a dual-axis line chart plots revenue on the left axis and transaction count on the right, so you can see whether the two move together.

<Frame caption="Revenue Impact report with summary cards and a dual-axis line chart.">
  <img src="/images/analytics/revenue-impact-final.png" alt="Revenue Impact report with line chart" />
</Frame>

## Staff Performance

Lists each staff member with six metrics for the selected period: stamps issued, points issued, rewards processed, members served, average stamps per day, and average points per day. Staff who had zero transactions in the window do not appear in the table.

<Frame caption="Staff Performance report showing per-staff metrics.">
  <img src="/images/analytics/staff-performance-final.png" alt="Staff Performance report table" />
</Frame>

Use this alongside the daily stamp count in Analytics Overview. If the overview shows a busy period but one staff member's numbers are flat, it usually means that person was absent, not that they underperformed.

## Location Comparison

Compares your branches on five metrics: stamps, points, redemptions, new members, and estimated revenue. Each location gets its own card. If you run a single-location business, you'll see one card.

<Frame caption="Location Comparison report showing per-branch performance cards.">
  <img src="/images/analytics/location-comparison-final.png" alt="Location Comparison report with location cards" />
</Frame>

<Info>
Location Comparison only shows branches that had at least one transaction in the selected period. A branch you opened recently or that was closed for part of the period may show fewer metrics than others.
</Info>

## Access

Reports follow the same permissions as the rest of Analytics. Managers have full access. For staff members, access depends on whether you granted them analytics permissions in their role settings.
