The Analytics page shows how your loyalty program is performing over time. It adapts to your program type — stamp card or points — and includes metrics, charts, AI-generated insights, regional benchmarks, and customer tools.

## Time Period Selector

At the top right of the Analytics page, choose how far back you want to look: **7 days**, **30 days**, **90 days**, or **12 months**. Every chart and metric on the page updates when you change this.

## Stamp Card Analytics

If your program uses stamp cards, the metrics grid shows:

- **Total Stamps** — stamps issued in the selected period, with change vs. the previous period
- **Redemptions** — rewards redeemed, with a redemption rate
- **New Members** — members who joined in the period
- **Return Rate** — the share of members who came back to scan more than once

Below the metrics, the charts section shows:

- A **Stamps over time** line chart with daily or weekly stamp volume
- A **Staff performance** bar chart showing stamps issued by each staff member
- A **Customer segments** donut chart grouping members by activity (loyal, at risk, new, and similar categories)
- A **Peak hours** heatmap showing which days and times see the most activity

## Points Program Analytics

If your program uses points, the metrics grid shows:

- **Points Earned** — points issued in the period, with change vs. the previous period
- **Points Redeemed** — points spent by members
- **Outstanding Points** — points issued but not yet spent
- **Avg Revenue/Point** — average transaction value per point issued

Below the metrics, the charts section shows:

- A **Points over time** line chart
- A **Points balance trend** showing how total outstanding points are moving
- A **Points expiry forecast** — points scheduled to expire in coming months
- **Top points earners** — members accumulating the most points
- **Reward popularity** — which rewards members redeem most

## Exporting Analytics

Click **Export** at the top right to download your data. The export dialog lets you pick which sections to include, then download as:

- **CSV** — raw data for the selected period
- **PDF** — a formatted report
- **Instagram Story** — a branded summary image sized for sharing

## Insights

The Insights panel surfaces automatically generated observations about your program. Each insight has a type — performance, trend, recommendation, warning, or opportunity — shown with a color-coded icon. Insights update as your data changes.

## AI Insights

Below the Insights panel, the AI Insights section provides a more detailed AI-generated analysis of your program. It identifies patterns across your data — such as unusual drops in activity or members approaching a reward — and presents them as a short written summary.

## Regional Benchmarks

The Regional Benchmarks panel compares your program's metrics against other businesses in your area. Each metric shows a percentile badge:

- **Top 25%** — outperforming 75% of similar businesses
- **Above Avg** — above the regional average
- **Below Avg** — below the regional average
- **Needs Work** — in the bottom 25% for this metric

This panel only appears when benchmark data is available for your region.

## Churn Risk

The Churn Risk panel lists members at risk of leaving your program. Each row shows the member name, risk level (high, medium, or low), the reason flagged, and the number of days since their last visit. Use this list to find members worth targeting with a campaign or direct message.

## Best Customers

The Best Customers panel shows your top 10 most active loyalty members. Each member carries a tier badge — platinum, gold, silver, or bronze — based on their activity. The list shows total visits and total points earned for each member.

## Ask AI About Your Data

The Ask AI panel at the bottom of the page lets you ask questions about your analytics in plain text. Suggested questions include:

- "Why did my stamps drop last week?"
- "How can I improve retention?"
- "What's my best performing day?"
- "How do I compare to other businesses?"

Type any question and the AI responds using your actual program data.

## Access

Owners and managers can open the Analytics page by default. Staff members do not have analytics access unless a manager or owner turns it on in their permission settings.
