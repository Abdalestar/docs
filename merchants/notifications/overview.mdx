The **Notifications** page is where you manage everything you've sent, queued, or drafted. You can finish a draft, edit a scheduled message before it goes out, check delivery stats on sent notifications, and build a library of templates for messages you use regularly.

To compose and send a new notification, see [Push Notifications](/merchants/campaigns/push-notifications).

## Tabs

**All** shows every notification regardless of status, newest first. Use this when you want to see the full picture without filtering.

**Drafts** lists notifications you saved without sending. Each draft card has a **Send** button so you can deliver it immediately without opening the compose form. Delete a draft from the three-dot menu on the card.

**Scheduled** shows notifications queued for a future time. You can delete a scheduled notification before it goes out; it won't send. After it sends, the card moves to Sent automatically.

**Sent** shows delivered notifications. Cards here are read-only. Each card shows the number of members who received the notification, the open rate, and the click rate.

**Templates** is a separate management interface for reusable messages. See [Managing templates](#managing-templates) below.

## Notification statuses

Each notification card displays a status badge that tells you where it is in its lifecycle.

<Frame caption="Notification lifecycle from draft through to sent or failed.">
  <img src="/images/notifications/notification-status-flow.svg" alt="Diagram showing notification states: Draft leads to Scheduled or Sending, which lead to Sent or Failed" />
</Frame>

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| Draft | Saved but not sent. You can edit it, send it, or delete it. |
| Scheduled | Queued to go out at a specific date and time. |
| Sending | Currently being delivered by OneSignal. |
| Sent | Delivered. Shows open and click rate stats. |
| Failed | OneSignal encountered a delivery error. |

## Delivery stats

Sent notification cards show four stats:

- **Sent**: the total number of members who received the notification
- **Delivered**: the percentage who the system confirmed received it
- **Opened**: the percentage who opened it
- **Clicked**: the percentage who tapped a link inside it

Stats update in real time as members interact.

## Managing templates

Templates save message drafts you can load when composing future notifications. They are shared across your whole organization.

<Frame caption="The Templates tab showing template cards with edit and delete actions.">
  <img src="/images/notifications/notifications-templates-final.png" alt="Templates tab with reusable message cards, each showing the template name, notification title, message preview, and edit and delete buttons" />
</Frame>

### Creating a template

1. Go to the **Templates** tab.
2. Click **New Template**.
3. Fill in three required fields and one optional field:
   - **Template Name**: an internal label for your own reference, not sent to customers. Example: "Weekend promo."
   - **Notification Title**: the bold headline of the notification. Maximum 100 characters.
   - **Message**: the body text. Maximum 500 characters.
   - **Image URL**: optional. A link to an image that appears below the message. Recommended size is 1440 x 720 px.
4. Click **Save Template**.

### Editing a template

Click the pencil icon on the template card. The same form opens with the saved values. Change what you need and save.

Editing a template does not affect notifications that were already sent using that template. Only future uses pick up the changes.

### Deleting a template

Click the trash icon on the template card. A confirmation dialog asks you to confirm before deleting. This cannot be undone.

System templates (created by Qtap, marked with a system badge) cannot be deleted.

### Using a template when composing

When composing a new notification, click **Use Template** in the header of the message form. A dropdown lists all your templates by name. Selecting one fills the Title, Message, and Image URL fields. You can edit the text before sending; the template itself is not affected.

## Access

Owners and managers can access the Notifications page. Staff members cannot, unless a custom permission explicitly grants them campaign access.
