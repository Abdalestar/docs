---
title: "Assigning Staff to Locations"
description: "Restrict where a staff member can issue stamps and redeem rewards."
---

When you have more than one location, every staff member is assigned to one or more of them. They can only stamp customers and redeem rewards at the locations they're assigned to. Owners and Managers can stamp and redeem anywhere by default.

## How assignment works

When you invite a new staff member from **Staff → Add Staff**, the form has a **Locations** field. Pick one or more locations. The staff member is assigned to those.

If you only have one location, this field doesn't appear. The single location is assigned automatically.

## Changing assignments later

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the staff member">
    Go to **Staff** in the left sidebar. Click the row of the staff member you want to update.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Edit">
    The staff member's profile opens. Click **Edit** at the top right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update Locations">
    Tick or untick the locations you want to assign. You can pick more than one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save Changes**. The staff member's access updates immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What restricted access actually blocks

If a Staff role is assigned to Location A only, they will:

- Be able to stamp customers and redeem rewards while logged in at Location A.
- See members and pending rewards filtered to Location A on the Redemptions screen.
- Get a “This reward is for a different location” error if they try to redeem a reward earned at Location B.

Managers and Owners are not restricted by location, even if you set a Locations field on their profile. They can stamp and redeem anywhere.

## Multi-location staff

A staff member can be assigned to as many locations as you like. This is common for relief staff or shift managers who move between branches. Their actions are tagged to whichever location they're stamping at, based on the QR code they scan.

## How Qtap knows which location a stamp is at

QR codes carry the location with them. When a customer scans a QR code at Location A, the resulting stamp is tagged to Location A, regardless of which staff member generated the QR code. NFC tags work the same way.

For manual stamps, staff pick the location from a dropdown on the manual-stamp screen. The dropdown only shows locations they're assigned to.
