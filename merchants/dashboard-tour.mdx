## How it's organised

The diagram below shows all nine sections and what each covers. The sidebar stays visible on every page so you can jump between them without losing your place.

## Home

Four numbers appear at the top: total members, active members (last 30 days), stamps or points issued, and rewards redeemed. Below that, the Activity Overview chart plots your stamps or points over the past 30 days alongside redemptions. Quick Actions links to the most common tasks — creating a card, generating a QR code, starting a campaign, or inviting a staff member.

<Warning>
Those four numbers are not real-time. They update daily. For live transaction data, check the Recent Activity feed at the bottom of the home page.
</Warning>

If your account is still in a trial, a Trial Impact card appears above the stats showing how many customers you've enrolled so far.

## Stamp Cards or Points

If you signed up for stamps, the sidebar shows **Cards**. If you chose points, it shows **Points**. Either way, this is where you build and manage your loyalty program: card designs, stamp goals, point values, and the rewards customers earn.

<Frame caption="The Stamp Cards page showing two active cards. Points-based accounts see a similar view under Points.">
  <img src="/images/getting-started/dashboard-tour-cards.png" alt="Stamp Cards page showing two active stamp card designs" />
</Frame>

## Members

Members lists everyone enrolled in your program. You can search by name, phone number, or email, view a customer's full transaction history, and manually issue stamps or points when needed. Most of the time your staff issue stamps by scanning the customer's QR code in the mobile app; the Members page is for exceptions and lookups.

<Frame caption="The Members list showing enrolled customers and their activity.">
  <img src="/images/getting-started/dashboard-tour-members.png" alt="Members list with customer names, join dates, and stamp counts" />
</Frame>

## QR Codes and NFC Tags

These are the two ways customers check in. QR Codes generates unique scannable codes you print or display at your counter. NFC Tags lets you register physical tap stickers: customers tap their phone to earn stamps or points without opening the app. Most merchants put a QR code at the till and NFC tags near the entrance or on tables.

## Campaigns

Campaigns sends automated messages when customers hit milestones or go quiet. You configure the trigger once. Qtap handles the sending.

Three campaign types cover most situations: a **birthday treat** that goes out on a customer's birthday, a **win-back** that fires after a set number of days without a visit, and a **milestone reward** that kicks in when a customer reaches a stamp or points target.

## Analytics

Analytics shows how your program is performing over time. You can see which locations are busiest, how many stamps or points were issued versus redeemed, and whether revenue from loyalty customers is trending up or down. The default view covers the last 30 days; you can adjust the date range.

<Frame caption="The Analytics overview showing member activity and stamp issuance trends.">
  <img src="/images/getting-started/dashboard-tour-analytics.png" alt="Analytics page with charts for stamps issued, active members, and revenue impact" />
</Frame>

## Staff

Staff lists everyone who can access the dashboard or the mobile scanning app. You invite team members by email, assign each one a role (owner, manager, or staff), and set which parts of the dashboard they can reach. Staff on the basic role can issue stamps and points from the mobile app but cannot change program settings or view analytics.

<Frame caption="The Staff page showing team members and their roles.">
  <img src="/images/getting-started/dashboard-tour-staff.png" alt="Staff list with member names, roles, and last active dates" />
</Frame>

## Settings

Settings covers four areas: Billing (your plan and payment method), Locations (add or edit branches), Notifications (which alerts go to which staff), and Merchant Page (the public profile customers see in the mobile app). If you need to upgrade from your trial or add a new location, this is where you start.
