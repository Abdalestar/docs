This page explains the words you'll see across the Qtap dashboard. If you've never run a loyalty program before, read this first.

## Loyalty program

The rules you set for rewarding repeat customers. In Qtap, a loyalty program is either a **stamp card** or a **points program**. You pick one type per program. You can run more than one program at a time, depending on your plan.

## Stamp card

A digital version of the paper card with circles you stamp at a coffee shop. The customer earns one stamp per visit (or per purchase, if you choose). When they reach the **stamp goal** you set, they earn the reward.

Use a stamp card when the rule is simple, like "buy 10 coffees, get one free."

## Points program

Customers earn points instead of stamps. You decide how many points each visit or purchase is worth, and you set up reward tiers (for example, 100 points = free pastry, 250 points = free meal).

Use a points program when you want flexibility. Customers can save up for bigger rewards, and you can offer multiple reward levels.

## Stamp goal

The number of stamps a customer needs to earn the **main reward** on a stamp card. The default is 10. You can change this in the card setup.

## Reward

What the customer gets in return for stamps or points. A reward has a name ("Free Coffee"), an optional image, and an optional expiry date.

Three types of rewards exist on stamp cards:

- **Main reward** — given when the customer reaches the stamp goal.
- **Sign Up reward** — given the first time a customer joins the card.
- **Interim reward** — given at a stamp number you choose, before the goal. Useful for keeping customers engaged on long cards.

## Member

A customer who has joined one of your loyalty programs. Members have a profile in your dashboard with their name, contact details, stamp or point balances, and visit history.

## Redemption

When a member uses their reward. The reward shows in the member's app as a code or QR code. Your staff verifies the code and marks the reward as redeemed.

## Campaign

An automatic message sent to a group of members. You set the trigger (a birthday, no visits in 30 days, etc.) and Qtap handles the timing. A campaign can include a reward.

## QR code

A scannable square barcode. In Qtap, QR codes are used for two things:

- **Earning** — a member opens their app, scans your QR code, and gets a stamp or points.
- **Redeeming** — a member shows the code on their reward, and a staff scans it to mark the reward as used.

## NFC tag

A small physical chip that members tap with their phone, instead of scanning a QR code. Same idea, different hardware. Useful at a counter where the phone is held flat.

## Location

One physical branch of your business. If you only have one shop, you have one location. Each location can have its own QR codes, its own staff, and its own analytics view.

## Staff

The people who use the dashboard with you. Each staff member has a role (Owner, Manager, or Staff) and can be assigned to specific locations. Staff with the right permissions can stamp customers, redeem rewards, and look up members.

## Plan

Your Qtap subscription tier. The plan controls how many locations, staff seats, loyalty cards, and QR scans you can use each month. The plans are Starter, Growth, Elite, and Franchise. See [Plans Overview](/merchants/billing/plans-overview).

## Add-on

An extra you can buy on top of your plan. Add-ons include extra locations, extra loyalty cards, NFC tags, and AI insight credits.

## Trial

Your first 14 days on Qtap. The trial gives you everything in the Growth plan. After 14 days, your card is charged automatically unless you cancel.
