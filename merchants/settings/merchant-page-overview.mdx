The **Merchants** page shows your public business profile — the page customers land on when they find your business through the Qtap app. You can create it, preview it, edit it, and delete it from here.

Only the account owner can access this page.

## No profile yet

If you have not created a profile, the page shows an empty state with a single **Create Merchant** button.

Click **Create Merchant** to open the profile editor. See [Merchant Page settings](/merchants/settings/merchant-page) for a field-by-field guide to everything in that form.

## The profile card

Once a profile exists, the page shows a card with the details from your settings.

The card displays your logo (or a placeholder if you have not uploaded one), your business name with an **Active** badge, and any of these that you filled in: description, category, location, phone, website, and Google rating. Fields you left blank are hidden rather than showing as empty.

Below those details, four counters update from your live loyalty program activity: total members, stamps earned, points earned, and redemptions. The stamps, points, and redemptions counters only appear once at least one transaction has been recorded. A brand-new account shows just the member count.

## Actions

Three buttons sit on the right side of the card.

**Preview** opens your public merchant page in a new tab, exactly as customers see it. This button only appears after you save a URL slug for your profile in the profile editor.

**Edit** opens the profile editor — the same form you used when creating the profile. Any changes you save take effect right away.

**Delete** removes your profile entirely. A confirmation dialog warns that deleting removes your cover photo, logo, and all merchant settings with no way to recover them. If you are planning to update the profile rather than close your Qtap presence, choose Edit instead.

<Warning>
Deleting your merchant profile takes your business offline in the Qtap app. Customers cannot find you through Qtap until you create a new profile.
</Warning>
