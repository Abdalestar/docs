The Notifications page controls two things: which emails Qtap sends to your inbox, and the message customers see when the app asks them for push permission. Go to **Settings → Notifications** in the left sidebar.

## Email Preferences

This section lists every type of email Qtap can send to your account email. Each row has an on/off toggle.

- **Billing Notifications** — receipts, payment failures, plan changes. Default: on. Worth keeping on, since payment failures need a quick reaction.
- **Weekly AI Digest** — a Sunday morning summary of how your loyalty program performed last week, with notes on members at risk of leaving and ideas for what to do next. Default: on.
- **Campaign Alerts** — a heads-up when a scheduled campaign sends, and a summary after a campaign finishes. Default: on.
- **Payment Failure Alerts** — a separate, urgent email if your card is declined. Default: on. Cannot be turned off while you have an active subscription.

Click **Save Preferences** at the bottom of the section to keep your changes.

<Note>
  We're rolling out email templates one at a time. The Weekly AI Digest is live now. Other emails are being added soon. Until then, the toggles are visible but only the digest is sending.
</Note>

## Push Notification Settings

This section controls how customers are asked for push permission inside your branded app, and the welcome message they see.

- **Push Enabled** — master toggle. If you turn this off, no campaign sends a push notification, even if you've configured one. Default: on.
- **Opt-In Prompt Message** — the text shown the first time a customer is asked to enable notifications. Default: “Enable notifications to get rewards and updates.” Keep it short and friendly.
- **Opt-In Prompt Subtitle** — a smaller line under the prompt. Optional.

## What sends what

A quick map of who controls which message:

- **You** control the messages your members see (campaign notifications, push prompt).
- **Qtap** controls billing and account-level messages to you.

## Testing your push prompt

The Opt-In Prompt is shown once per device. To test changes on your own phone, uninstall and reinstall the app, or clear app data, then open the app fresh.
