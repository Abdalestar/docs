If you can't remember your password and can't sign in, you don't need to contact support. Qtap emails you a reset link, and you set a new password from that link. The whole thing takes about a minute.

This is different from the **Change password** form on the [Security page](/merchants/settings/security). That form is for changing a password you already know while you're signed in. Use this page when you're locked out.

## Ask for a reset link

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Forgot Password page">
    On the login page, click **Forgot password?** under the password field. You land on the Forgot Password page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your email">
    Type the email address you use to sign in to Qtap, then click **Send Reset Link**.

    <Frame caption="Enter your sign-in email and click Send Reset Link.">
      <img src="/images/settings/password-reset-forgot.png" alt="Forgot Password form with the email field and the Send Reset Link button highlighted." />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your email">
    A **Check Your Email** screen appears, and Qtap sends an email titled "Reset your Qtap password" with the reset link inside. Open the email and click the link to continue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The Check Your Email screen shows up whether or not the address has a Qtap account. Qtap does this on purpose so nobody can use the form to guess which emails are registered. If no email arrives after a few minutes, check your spam folder, then confirm you typed the same address you signed up with.
</Note>

## Set your new password

The reset link signs you in for one purpose: choosing a new password. It opens the Reset Password page.

<Frame caption="Enter a new password, confirm it, then click Reset Password.">
  <img src="/images/settings/password-reset-new.png" alt="Reset Password form with the New Password field, Confirm Password field, and Reset Password button highlighted." />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Type a new password">
    Enter your new password in **New Password**. It needs at least 8 characters and has to include an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, and a number. This is stricter than the in-app change-password rule, so pick something that meets all four conditions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm it">
    Type the same password again in **Confirm Password**. If the two don't match, you'll see "Passwords do not match" and nothing is saved until they do.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Reset Password**. A **Password Updated** screen confirms the change, and **Continue to Dashboard** takes you straight in with your new password.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Reset links expire. If you open an old link or the link has already been used, the page can't save a new password. Go back to the login page and request a fresh link with **Forgot password?**
</Warning>

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I never got the reset email.">
    Check your spam or junk folder first. If it's still missing, the most likely cause is a typo or a different address than the one on your account. Request the link again and double-check the email you enter. The address has to match the one you sign in with.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The reset link says it's expired or invalid.">
    Each link works once and only for a limited time. Request a new one from the **Forgot password?** link on the login page, then use the newest email.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I remember my password and want to change it.">
    Sign in, then use the **Change password** form on the [Security page](/merchants/settings/security). That form asks for your current password first, so you don't need a reset link.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do staff reset their own passwords this way?">
    Yes. Anyone with a Qtap login, owners and staff alike, uses the same Forgot password flow. It works on the login page before anyone signs in.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
