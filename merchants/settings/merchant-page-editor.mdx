Your merchant page is the public profile a customer lands on when they scan your QR code or open your business in the Qtap app. It shows your cover photo, logo, business details, and the loyalty cards they can join. The Merchant Page editor is where you build it, and a live phone preview on the right updates as you type, so you can see the page before you publish it.

## Open the editor

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Merchants">
    Click **Merchants** in the left sidebar. This is your business overview.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the page designer">
    Click **Create Merchant** to build a page for the first time, or **Edit Merchant** if you already have one. Either opens the editor.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The editor is owner-only. Managers and staff see an access-denied screen, even with other dashboard permissions.
</Note>

## Your business profile

The **Merchant Profile** section sets how your business appears at the top of the page.

<Frame caption="The Merchant Profile section: cover photos (1), Google rating (2), and category (3), plus your name, logos, and description.">
  <img src="/images/merchants/merchant-page/merchant-page-profile.png" alt="The Merchant Profile section with cover photos, Google rating, and category boxed" />
</Frame>

- **Merchant Name** is your business name as customers read it.
- **Cover Photos**: add up to five, in PNG or JPEG, each under 5MB. Drag to reorder. The first one is your main cover and the rest are supplementary.
- **Merchant Logo**: a square image, PNG or JPEG, up to 10MB.
- **Merchant Logo (Transparent PNG)**: a square transparent PNG at 512 by 512 pixels, up to 10MB. Qtap uses it as the stamp icon on mobile loyalty cards.
- **Description** is a short line about your business.
- **Google Rating** is a slider from 0 to 5. You set this number by hand. It does not read from Google, so keep it honest and update it yourself.
- **Category** is one of eight: Restaurants, Cafes, Retail, Beauty, Health, Services, Entertainment, or Other.

## Location details

The **Location Details** section holds the practical information customers need to find and reach you: Location Name, Address, Phone Number, Working Hours, and Website URL. Working Hours is a free text field, so you can write it however suits your business.

## The loyalty cards you feature

The **Loyalty Cards** section picks which of your published programs show on the page. These are your real cards, so a customer who joins from the page starts collecting straight away.

<Frame caption="Featuring a loyalty card. Tap a card to feature it (1), or add a new one (2).">
  <img src="/images/merchants/merchant-page/merchant-page-loyalty.png" alt="The Loyalty Cards section with a card and the Create a loyalty card button boxed" />
</Frame>

Tap a card to feature it; a featured card is highlighted. Tap it again to remove it. If you have no published card yet, use **Create a loyalty card** to make one first, then come back and feature it here.

## Save, preview, and reset

The buttons at the bottom act on the whole page.

<Frame caption="The page actions. Create or update the page (1), or open a live preview in a new tab (2).">
  <img src="/images/merchants/merchant-page/merchant-page-actions.png" alt="The Create Merchant and Preview buttons boxed" />
</Frame>

- **Create Merchant** saves your page for the first time. After that the same button reads **Update Merchant** and saves your changes.
- **Preview** opens your page in a new browser tab, exactly as customers see it, using your current edits. You do not have to save first, so it is a safe way to check the page before you publish.
- **Delete** appears once you have a page. It resets the page back to empty by clearing the cover photo, logo, and merchant page settings.

<Warning>
Delete only resets the merchant page. Your loyalty programs, members, and their collected stamps and points are not touched.
</Warning>
